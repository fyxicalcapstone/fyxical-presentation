High Variability in Care
Two clinicians treating the same condition may prescribe entirely different plans — leading to unpredictable outcomes, payer skepticism, and patient frustration.
Manual & Time-Consuming
Clinicians rely on memory, habit, and personal preference. Exercise libraries are large but not intelligently searchable — creating cognitive load and workflow friction.
No Real-Time Personalization
Static treatment templates cannot adapt to how a patient actually responds. Chronic patients — 38.4% of our cohort — are the most costly and benefit most from adaptive logic.
Equity Gap
Manual labor workers show 25.6% "crippled" ODI rates vs. 17.7% for acute patients. Care disparities across occupation, age, and demographics go undetected without monitoring.
Our Solution

Fyxical: AI-Driven Exercise Pathway Engine for Outpatient PT

A health-tech decision-support platform that matches patients to evidence-informed exercise pathways at intake — keeping the therapist in the loop while eliminating guesswork.

🔬
Patient Similarity Engine
Multi-dimensional matching on demographics, diagnosis codes, baseline function, comorbidities, and prior response trajectories

Rule + ML Engine

POC: Rule Engine + Supervised ML Validation

Clinician Acceptance (Target) ≥70% (Ph.2)

4 Treatment Pathways Assigned
Acute Mild/Moderate — pain <2 wks, VAS <7, ODI <40%
Acute Severe — pain <2 wks, VAS ≥7 or ODI ≥40%
Subacute — pain 2–12 weeks
Chronic — pain >12 wks, ODI ≥60% → most intensive
Clinical Validation Insight
Strong positive correlation confirmed: no mild-pain patient maps to "crippled" — pain severity perfectly predicts disability tier in the synthetic cohort, validating rule engine logic.
Honest Limitation
LR labels are rule-derived, not independent clinical outcomes. Phase 3 resolves this with real follow-up data. POC scope: feasibility, not clinical prediction validity.

Scale-Up Roadmap

From POC to Clinical Trial — 4-Phase Plan

Phase 2 · 3–6 Months — Outcome Infrastructure
Expand dataset with real/pilot follow-up outcomes (pain score Δ, ROM, ODI deltas). Add progression pathway logic. IRB approval initiated concurrently. Existing BigQuery + Vertex AI tables already structured for expansion.
Phase 3 · 6–12 Months — Supervised Model & Clinician Validation
Train on real clinical outcome labels (not rule-derived). Introduce cost-sensitive metrics penalizing false downgrades 3–5× more than false upgrades. Conduct PT review (Cohen's Kappa ≥70% agreement target). Enroll n=300 pilot patients.
Phase 4 · 12–24 Months — Multi-Clinic Deployment & NIH Clinical Trial
Deploy across 2+ outpatient PT sites. Continuous model retraining with outcome feedback loops. Equity monitoring dashboards live. Formal NIH Phase I trial: n=500+ patients, 6-month follow-up, peer-reviewed publication.
Long-Term · 24–36 Months — Commercial Scale & SaMD Pathway
EHR API integrations (HL7 FHIR / LOINC). HIPAA BAA GCP transition. FDA SaMD lifecycle management. Payer value-based care partnerships. Head of Commercial + Regulatory Lead hired.
