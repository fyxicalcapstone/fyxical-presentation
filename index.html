Company Overview

What Fyxical Does

Fyxical is a health-tech startup that helps outpatient orthopedic physical therapists design and adapt individualized treatment plans using real-time data, advanced algorithms, and clinician input.

For 30 years, the evidence has shown that PT for chronic pain delivers small, inconsistent results. Fyxical transforms that reality by bringing data‑driven personalization and therapist‑centric decision support to a field that has never had it.

Value Proposition
Faster, more personalized plans — suggestions based on how similar patients have responded.
Continuous optimization — recommendations evolve with each patient interaction, reducing guesswork.
Therapist in the loop — system augments clinical judgment rather than replacing it.
Measurable outcomes — built-in tracking links interventions to functional improvements.

Known Gaps & Next Steps
Current pathways are built on expert-informed rules rather than independently observed outcomes — expanding the dataset with real-world follow-up data is the next milestone that will strengthen predictive validity.
Scale-Up Roadmap

From POC to Clinical Trial — 4-Phase Plan

Phase 2 · 3–6 Months — Outcome Infrastructure
Expand the dataset to include real or pilot-collected follow-up outcomes: pain score changes, range of motion measurements, session attendance, and Modified Oswestry score changes. Add progression logic so patients can move between pathways based on their response. The existing BigQuery tables, Vertex AI notebooks, and Looker Studio dashboard are already structured to support this expansion.
Phase 3 · 6–12 Months — Outcome-Informed Model & Validation
Train the model on independently observed outcome data. Unlike the POC, these labels will come from real patient results — making model performance metrics genuinely meaningful for the first time. The evaluation approach will prioritize patient safety by weighting under-treatment errors more heavily. Conduct independent clinician review of pathway assignments. Enroll n=300 pilot patients.
Phase 4 · 12–24 Months — Multi-Clinic Deployment & NIH Trial
Deploy across multiple clinic locations with outcome monitoring and continuous model improvement through clinician feedback. Equity monitoring dashboards will detect and address any disparate pathway assignment rates across demographic groups. Formal NIH Phase I trial: n=500+ patients, 6-month follow-up, targeting peer-reviewed publication.
Long-Term · 24–36 Months — Commercial Scale & Regulatory Pathway
Integrate with electronic health record systems and payer networks. Pursue regulatory clearance pathways. Build value-based care partnerships. Head of Commercial Partnerships and Regulatory Lead hired per the team hiring roadmap.
